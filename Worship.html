The myth of the sun and moon, a component of celestial mythology, occupies a very important position in the history of mythology. The sun and moon are not only natural phenomena that people most easily access to, but also have a very close relationship with people's real life (production, living place and physical health, etc.).

There is rich history and evidence of how ancient Chinese observe the sky, worship the sun and moon.

WORSHIP.

It starts with the observation. Through observation, ancient Chinese people realize the close relationship between time and sun. They use gnomon to record time.

Not only observing the sun, according to legend, China had an observatory in the Xia Dynasty(Circa 2070 BC. - Circa 1600 BC), which was called "Qing Tai" at that time. Chinese emperors believed that they were ordered by heaven to rule the world, In order to protect the regime, the emperor interpreted astronomical visions as the will of heaven and the way of heaven, so he paid great attention to astronomical observation.

From the Yuan Dynasty to the sixteenth year of the Yuan Dynasty (1279), Kublai Khan established a large-scale observatory in the capital (today's Beijing), and the national astronomical institution - the Imperial History Institute is located here, by the Yuan astronomer Guo Shoujing created a number of astronomical instruments. The observatory was scientifically constructed and well equipped, and could be said to be one of the most advanced observatories in the world at that time.

There was Astronomical calculation. According to the Pythagorean theorem and the proportion method, ancient Mathematician calculates the distance between sun and the city, also the diameter of the sun.

Shang and Zhou Sun God bird gold ornaments for the Shang and Zhou period of gold. It was unearthed in Jinsha Village in 2001 and is now collected in Chengdu Jinsha Site Museum. Shang Zhou Sun God Bird gold jewelry as a whole is a circular sheet, the outer diameter is 12.5 cm, the inner diameter is 5.29 cm, the thickness is 0.02 cm, the weight is 20 grams. The pattern is divided into inner and outer layers, and the inner layer is equally spaced with twelve rotating tooth-like rays; The outer layer consists of four identical birds flying counterclockwise.
